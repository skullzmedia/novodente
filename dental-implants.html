What are Dental Implants?

Dental implants are a modern, long term solution for replacing missing teeth. They consist of a durable titanium post that is surgically placed into the jawbone, acting as an artificial tooth root. Over time, the bone naturally integrates with the implant, creating a strong, stable foundation for a custom-made crown, bridge, or denture.

Key Components of Dental Implants

  • Titanium Post: The replacement root that securely anchors the implant in the jawbone.
  • Abutment: A connector placed on the implant to hold the final restoration.
  • Restoration: A custom-made crown, bridge, or denture that restores function and appearance.
